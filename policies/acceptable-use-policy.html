Acceptable Use Policy

Last updated: January 16, 2026

This Acceptable Use Policy ("AUP") describes prohibited activities when using the Dodo Checks Service. This policy helps protect our customers, partners, and recipients.

1) You must use the Service lawfully

You may not use the Service in violation of any law or regulation, including laws related to payments, fraud, money laundering, sanctions, consumer protection, and privacy.

2) Prohibited activities

You may not, and may not allow others to:

  • Submit checks or payment instructions that are fraudulent, misleading, or unauthorized.
  • Use the Service to facilitate money laundering, bribery, or other financial crime.
  • Attempt to evade sanctions or transact with sanctioned persons or prohibited jurisdictions.
  • Impersonate any person or entity or misrepresent your affiliation.
  • Upload malware, attempt to bypass security controls, or probe for vulnerabilities.
  • Interfere with the Service (e.g., denial-of-service attempts, automated scraping).
  • Access or use another user’s account without permission.
  • Upload or disclose personal information you do not have the right to share (for example, sharing a payee’s data without authorization).
  • Use the Service to harass, threaten, or cause harm to others.

3) Data accuracy and authorization

You are responsible for confirming that each check, payee, and mailing address is accurate and that you have the legal right to issue the payment and provide associated data.

4) Monitoring and enforcement

We may investigate suspected violations and take action, including suspending or terminating accounts, delaying fulfillment, refusing orders, and reporting activity to appropriate authorities, as permitted by law.

5) Contact

If you believe someone is misusing the Service, contact us at mailing@dodochecks.com.
